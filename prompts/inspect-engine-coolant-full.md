# GUIDED VIDEO INSPECTION INSTRUCTIONS - ENGINE COOLANT LEVEL
## Heavy Equipment Daily Video Analysis

### OVERVIEW
This guided inspection module provides comprehensive video analysis instructions for engine coolant level and cooling system components on heavy construction equipment (primarily excavators). This system identifies critical cooling system failures, moderate maintenance needs, and normal operational conditions related specifically to coolant levels, radiator systems, cooling system leaks, and temperature management.

---

## INSPECTION CATEGORIES AND DETECTION POINTS

### 🔴 CRITICAL SAFETY ISSUES (RED INDICATORS)
**Immediate Action Required - Equipment Should Not Operate**

#### **COOLANT LEVEL CRITICAL ISSUES**
- **Critically Low Coolant Levels**
  - Look for: Coolant below minimum marks, empty coolant reservoirs, visible low coolant warnings
  - Detection Points: Coolant level indicators below safe range, empty expansion tanks
  - Risk Level: Critical - Engine overheating and complete engine failure

- **Continuous Coolant Loss**
  - Look for: Daily coolant addition requirements, progressive coolant loss, system depletion
  - Risk Level: Critical - Cooling system failure and engine damage

#### **COOLANT SYSTEM LEAK FAILURES**
- **Major Coolant System Leaks**
  - Look for: Visible coolant leaks, puddles under equipment, fluid streaming
  - Risk Level: Critical - Cooling system failure and engine overheating

- **Radiator System Damage**
  - Look for: Damaged radiator guards, radiator core damage, protection system failure
  - Risk Level: Critical - Radiator damage and cooling system compromise

- **Cooling System Hose Failures**
  - Look for: Damaged cooling system hoses, loose hose clamps, connection failures
  - Risk Level: Critical - Cooling system pressure loss and overheating

#### **WATER PUMP AND COOLING COMPONENT FAILURES**
- **Water Pump Internal Failures**
  - Look for: Water pump leakage, internal seal failures, coolant contamination
  - Risk Level: Critical - Engine cooling failure and internal engine damage

- **Coolant Contamination Issues**
  - Look for: Coolant contamination in engine oil, cross-contamination
  - Risk Level: Critical - Engine lubrication system contamination and failure

#### **HEATING AND COOLING SYSTEM CONTROL FAILURES**
- **Climate Control System Failure**
  - Look for: Non-functioning heating/cooling systems
  - Risk Level: Critical - Operator comfort and safety

### 🟡 MODERATE ISSUES (YELLOW INDICATORS)
**Attention Required - Schedule Maintenance**

- **Coolant Level Maintenance Monitoring**
- **Coolant Quality Assessment**
- **Radiator Cleaning and Maintenance**
- **Cooling System Hose Monitoring**
- **Engine Temperature Monitoring**

### ✅ NORMAL CONDITIONS (GREEN INDICATORS)
**Acceptable Operating Condition**

- **Adequate Coolant Level**
- **Clean Coolant Condition**
- **Leak-Free Cooling System**
- **Functional Radiator System**
- **Effective Engine Cooling**
- **Functional Climate Control**

---

## COMMON FALSE POSITIVES TO AVOID

- Water or condensation vs. coolant leakage
- Environmental debris vs. coolant contamination
- Lighting conditions affecting level reading accuracy
- Previous maintenance residue vs. active leakage
- Normal coolant expansion and contraction with temperature
- Coolant level not visible due to obstructed view → note as low-severity
